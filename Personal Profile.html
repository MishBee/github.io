How these reflect on my personality are somewhat of interest and provide insight into what
may have been unknown about myself and a learning experience about oneself. The insights they
provide may help guide future direction and an opportunity to work on problem areas that may
hinder further development. The results were accurate in portraying my personality,
as with all personality types, the results declared my strengths and weaknesses, which I found
to be quite accurate, and to which I may utilise to my advantage.

The results show that I may not favour working within groups as have been declared to be quite introverted.
In saying that, I can work within groups, communicate ideas and perspectives when I have something that
may be a valuable contribution to the group. I may portray an air of reservation at first, but as I have
more experiences with peers and colleagues, my reserved nature seems to fade into the background,
and I feel more relaxed about being open with communication. The overall take on this would be that I
still have personal areas to improve to be able to a fully effective team member, and the journey of
self-improvement never ends.

Factors that should be considered when forming groups, would be to improve communication and take
a more proactive approach towards learning about fellow team members and to increase input into
project ideas. Being grouped with more extroverted personalities would ease communication and
collaboration would be more effective due to openness of other team members, encouraging me to be more open myself.
